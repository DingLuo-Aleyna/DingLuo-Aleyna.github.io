Art Basel Hong Kong 2025

Asian Galleries Represent 45.8%, Signaling Shift to Asia

2025.3.31

CHEN Ruqing, WANG Yunqi

Art Basel Hong Kong 2025 closed on March 30, marking the significant shift in the global art scene. This year, Asian galleries made up 45.8% of all exhibitors, while European and North American numbers slipped.

Art Basel Hong Kong 2025 attracted 240 galleries from 42 countries and regions, maintaining a scale comparable to its 2024 edition. It is a clear sign that Art Basel is back in full swing after the 2020 cancellation.

Notably, nearly half of the exhibitors are from Asia. Compared to the pre-pandemic 2019, Asian galleries at Art Basel Hong Kong have increased their share from 41.7% to 45.8%, demonstrating the vibrant growth of Asia's art scene. In contrast, European galleries saw their participation drop from 37.6% to 34.2%, while North American representation experienced a slight 1.1% decline.

The expansion of Asian galleries reveals the strategic set and significant anticipation by Art Basel. As highlighted by the Art Basel and UBS Global Collections Survey 2024, Chinese high-net-worth individuals maintain the world's highest art spending:

According to the press release of the Art Basel 2025, HNWIs reported the highest spend on art and antiques in 2023 and the first half of 2024, more than double any other region. This further bolsters our positive outlook for this March.

The Asian art scene is reaching impressive new heights, not only in terms of participating gallery numbers but also in the quality of artworks being showcased.

Five of the fair's ten selected 'Top 10 Galleries' came from Asia, including one collaborates with French partners. This strong representation serves as a powerful endorsement from both attendees and selection committees, highlighting Asia's growing influence in the global art world.

The rising presence of Asian galleries and the exceptional quality of artworks this year highlight an eastward shift in the art market. This evolution is echoed in the conclusion press release issued by Art Basel Hong Kong 2025: “This year’s edition not only featured a diverse and innovative program but also underscored our deep commitment to nurturing Asia’s thriving art ecosystem.”
