Art Basel HK 2025

Asian Galleries Represent 45.8%, Signaling Shift to Asia

2025.3.31

CHEN Ruqing , WANG Yunqi

Art Basel Hong Kong 2025 closed on March 30, demonstrating a significant rebalancing in the global art market. Asian galleries achieved representation at 45.8% of all exhibitors, while participation from both Europe and North America showed declines. The numbers confirm an accelerating shift of the art world's center of gravity toward Asia.

This year, Art Basel HK attracted 240 galleries from 42 countries and regions, maintaining a scale comparable to its 2024 edition - a strong signal of the art market's full recovery since the 2020 cancellation.

Nearly half of this year's exhibitors at Art Basel HK hail from Asia, a strategic emphasis the fair's organizers explicitly acknowledge. “ underscore the fair’s commitment to championing the region's artistic voices," Art Basel stated on its official platform.

The 2025 edition arrives with heightened expectations following Art Basel and UBS's Global Art Market Report 2024, which revealed mainland China's high-net-worth individuals (HNWIs) maintain the world's highest median art spending:

Compared to the pre-pandemic 2019 figure, Asian galleries at Art Basel HK have increased their share from 41.7% to 45.8%, demonstrating the vibrant growth of Asia's art scene. In contrast, European galleries saw their participation drop from 37.6% to 34.2%, while North American representation experienced a slight 1.1% decline.

Leading blue-chip galleries (such as Gagosian and David Zwirner) continue to dominate prime exhibition spaces. Meanwhile, smaller galleries from Europe and North America face developmental challenges amid growing competition from Asia-Pacific counterparts.

The Asia-Pacific region has not only reached new highs in gallery representation but has also achieved remarkable quality in artworks. Among the fair's official "Top 10 Galleries" selection, five hail from Asia - clear recognition by both visitors and Art Basel's organizing committee of the region's qualitative artistic advancement.

The increased representation of Asian galleries at Art Basel HK 2025 confirms the eastward shift in the art market's center of gravity. As Angelle Siyang-Le, Director of Art Basel Hong Kong, stated: "We remain dedicated to supporting the future of the contemporary scene in Asia."
